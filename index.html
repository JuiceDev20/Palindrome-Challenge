Palindrome Challenge

A palindrome is a word, phrase, number or sequence of words that reads the same backward as forward. Punctuation and spaces between the words or lettering is allowed.

The most common of English palindromes are those that are read character by character, for instance, "level," "rotor," and "racecar". Character by character means that each character of the word matches and the word can be spelled the same forwards or backward. The word palindrome is derived from the Greek 'palin,' or "back" and 'dromos' or "direction." The actual Greek phrase alluded to the backward movement of the crab.
